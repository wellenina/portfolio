Lost Colors

Step into the home of a once-brilliant painter who, afflicted by a severe creative block, now sees the world in black and white - literally.

Armed with the magical Color Brush, it’s up to you to help them. Color and restore the objects depicted in their paintings, unlock new rooms, and ultimately rediscover the lost inspiration. With every puzzle you solve, color will slowly come back into their life.

Lost Colors is a project developed during the Online Master at Digital Bros Game Academy, in collaboration with a team of Game Programming and Game Design students, between February and April 2025.

It’s a first-person puzzle game for PC inspired by titles like The Unfinished Swan, Perfect Angle, Superliminal and Viewfinder.

Made with Unreal Engine 5 and C++

My contributions to the project

  • Temporary color stains appearing on objects (using decal materials)
  • Floating animation for key object fragments
  • Color mixing system that allows combining primary colors to create secondary colors
  • Sound effects, background music, and muffled audio effect during “Focus Mode”
  • Debugging and bug fixing, some code refactoring

One thing I’m pretty proud of

I developed a simple custom tool to position and scale key object fragments in 3D space, used to create a forced perspective illusion.

The tool performs a simple mathematical proportion between the initial distance from the point of view and the initial scale of the object, and the new distance and scale. The real challenge was designing a system that would be both intuitive for users with limited technical skills, and robust enough to avoid errors or unexpected behavior.

I’m happy that the designer team found it easy, practical, and effective to use.
