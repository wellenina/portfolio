Cats Invaders

A menacing flock of cats is invading the planet! (Probably looking for food, since they haven’t eaten for a couple of hours.) You can choose among 12 valiant warriors and 14 lethal weapons (mostly fruits and vegetables, but still dreaded by cats) to fight these feisty felines and save the world. It’s meow or never!

(Fun fact: a group of cats is actually called a clowder. Flock sounds funnier though.)

A catty version of the classic 1978 arcade game Space Invaders (スペースインベーダー) featuring infinitely scrolling cats, it’s made with LÖVE and purr-fect for every cat lover.

Cats Invaders is a personal project and the first full game I built, inspired by CS50’s Introduction to Game Development. This is where I first learned about delta time, finite state machines, collision detection (using Axis-Aligned Bounding Boxes), particle systems, and more.

I programmed everything from scratch and was also the sole responsible for the game design (that’s why it’s not particularly fun to play.)

Made with LÖVE and Lua

Notable features

  • The invaders (the cats) shoot various stuff at the player’s character, while the player can move horizontally and shoot daikons at them
  • Unlike the traditional finite grid of invaders that have to be defeated in order for the next grid to appear, this version features an infinite scrolling army of cats. Their speed and shooting frequency increase progressively as the game advances
  • The player can choose among different characters and weapons
  • High scores and player preferences are saved in local storage
  • Multiple languages are supported
